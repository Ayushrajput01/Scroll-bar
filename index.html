This article is about the automobile manufactactuer. For other uses,see Lmborghini (disambiguation).Automobili Lamborghini S.p.A. (italian pronuciation: [auto'mo:bili Lamborghini'gi:ni]) is an italian brand and manufacturer of luxury sports cars and SUVs based in Sant' Agata Bolognese.
The company is owned by the Volkswagen througth its subsidiary Audi.Feruccio Lamborghini(1916_1993),an italian manufacturing magnate, founded Automobili Ferruccio Lamborghini S.p.A. in 1963 to compete with Ferrari.
The company was noted for using a rear mid-engine, rear-wheel drive layout\. lamborghini grew rapidly during its first decade, but sales plunged in the wake of the 1973 worldwide financial downturn and the oil crisis. the firm's ownership changed three times after 1973 including a bankruptcy in 1973, including a bankruptcy in 1978. American Chrysler Coporation took control of Lamborghini in 1987 and sold it to Malaysian investment group Mycom Setdco and indonesian group V'power Corporation in 1994. in 1998, Mycom Setdco and V'Power sold Lamborghini to the Volkswangen Group where it was placed under the control of the group's Audi division. New products and model ;ines were introduced to the brand's portfolio and brought to the market and saw and increased productivity for the brand.
in the late 2000s, during the worldwide financial crisis and the subsequent economic crisis,Lamborghini's sales saw a drop of nearly 50per cent. This article is about the automobile manufactactuer.
